## Story Overview

**Fashion Model** — A model who does not look right appears on a film set. What she does to the crew is described precisely. This is one of Ito's most genuinely frightening creature designs.

**Hanging Blimp** — Enormous balloon heads float over a city, each one resembling a resident. The blimps are not benign. The horror is in what the blimps want.

**Greased** — A boy's obsessive hair grooming and what this produces in his relationship with his family.

**Painter** — An artist who paints predictions — futures that occur because he painted them, or that he paints because they will occur. The logic of the story is the horror.

**The Rib Woman** — A woman whose ribs protrude through her skin. The horror is that she agrees to display herself.

**Marionette Mansion** — A family that builds human-shaped automatons. What happens when visitors arrive.

**Futon** — The specific fear of familiar domestic objects. What the futon has been doing.

**Tomio: Red Turtleneck** — A man whose head becomes separated from his body but continues living. The turtleneck is not a solution.

**Back Alley** — A surgeon who operates in an alley. What he is doing and why people come to him.

## Art Style

This collection spans different periods of Ito's work, which makes the visual consistency across stories notable — his style has a recognizable character that persists across decades. The specific horror images in this collection (the fashion model's face, the hanging blimps) have become among his most widely reproduced work for reasons visible on the page.

## Cultural Context

Several stories engage with specifically Japanese domestic anxiety — the futon, the alley doctor, the family automaton-builders — while others (the fashion model, the hanging blimps) are universally accessible horror. The ratio is typical of his work.

## What I Love About It

Ito selected these stories himself, which means the selection is a form of self-portrait. He chose stories that demonstrate what he finds interesting about horror rather than what has been most commercially successful. That curatorial honesty is the collection's most interesting quality.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe Shiver as the Ito collection that functions as an introduction and as a permanent reference — both the entry point and the volume they return to most. Fashion Model and Hanging Blimp are consistently cited as the stories that best explain why he matters.
