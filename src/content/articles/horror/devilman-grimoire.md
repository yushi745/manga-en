## Story Overview

Devilman Grimoire reimagines the Devilman story in a new setting: a high school where the class representative Miki is drawn into a demonic conspiracy when her childhood friend Akira merges with the demon Amon to become Devilman. Unlike the original's cosmic scale, Grimoire has a more intimate school setting, though the demons are just as violent and the stakes eventually expand to world-ending territory.

## Characters

Akira and Miki are recognizable from the original but with updated personalities appropriate to the contemporary school setting. The demon designs are inventive and disturbing. The story introduces new characters who don't appear in the original, giving it some surprises even for Devilman veterans.

## Art Style

Rui Takato's art is slick and contemporary — more mainstream seinen than Go Nagai's raw 1970s style. The action sequences are dynamic and the demon designs are genuinely creative. If you come expecting Nagai's vintage aesthetic, you'll need to adjust your expectations.

## Cultural Context

Go Nagai's original Devilman (1972) was a landmark of darker manga — combining action, horror, and tragedy in ways that shocked readers and influenced generations of creators. Netflix's Devilman Crybaby brought the story to international audiences. Grimoire is part of an ongoing project to keep Nagai's creation relevant across eras.

## What I Love About It

I have complicated feelings about Devilman Grimoire. The original Devilman broke me emotionally — its ending is one of the most devastating in manga history. Grimoire cannot replicate that because it's working within established mythology where readers know what's coming. But taken on its own terms, as dark action horror, it delivers.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Reviews from international readers are mixed. Devilman purists find Grimoire too sanitized compared to the original's transgressive extremity. Readers who come fresh find it a solid dark action horror manga. The consensus is that it's good but not essential except for franchise completists.
