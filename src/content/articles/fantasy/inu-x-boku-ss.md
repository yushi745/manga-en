## Story Overview

Ririchiyo Shirakiin has always said the wrong thing. She has a sharp tongue she can't control, and it has kept everyone at a distance — which she tells herself is what she wants, and which is a lie.

Maison de Ayakashi is an apartment complex for people with supernatural heritage — those who are part youkai, half-demon, or otherwise not fully human. Ririchiyo moves in hoping to practice being a normal person without the social weight of her family name.

Soushi Miketsukami is her Secret Service agent. He is a nine-tailed fox spirit of extraordinary power who is impossibly devoted to Ririchiyo — not as performance, not as manipulation. His devotion is real and it is rooted in a history between them that Ririchiyo doesn't initially remember.

The early volumes are comedy-romance about Ririchiyo slowly learning to connect with people. The later volumes reveal what Soushi actually is and why he loves her, and the series turns into something more affecting than it initially appears.

## Characters

**Ririchiyo Shirakiin** — A protagonist whose sharp tongue is a specific and recognizable defense mechanism — she drives people away before they can leave, and the series documents her learning that this isn't protection. Her growth across 11 volumes is genuine.

**Soushi Miketsukami** — The devoted guardian whose emotions are entirely real despite appearing excessive — the series earns his devotion by explaining its origin, which makes him more interesting than the simply-loyal male lead.

**The apartment residents** — An ensemble of half-youkai personalities whose relationships with each other create community for Ririchiyo to slowly enter.

## Art Style

Fujiwara's art suits the supernatural romance — youkai forms that are visually distinctive without being overwhelming, character designs that convey personality immediately, and expressive artwork that handles both the comedy and the genuine emotional moments. The art improves across the series.

## Cultural Context

Inu x Boku SS ran in Monthly GFantasy from 2009 to 2012. The youkai heritage concept draws from Japanese folklore while using it as metaphor for what it means to not fully belong to any single world — both Ririchiyo and Soushi are caught between human and supernatural, and their relationship is partly about finding someone who exists in the same in-between space.

## What I Love About It

The series' second half. When the dark turn arrives — when the full picture of what connects Ririchiyo and Soushi is revealed — everything from the first half recontextualizes. The comedy was always covering something more serious, and Fujiwara committed to it rather than retreating. The reincarnation arc requires reader investment from the first volume to land, and it does.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe Inu x Boku SS as a hidden gem of supernatural romance — specifically noted for the later volumes being significantly more affecting than the early volumes suggest, for Soushi's devotion being justified rather than simply given, and for Ririchiyo's growth arc being one of the more complete character developments in romance manga. Frequently described as underseen.
