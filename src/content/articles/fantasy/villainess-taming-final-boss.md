## Story Overview

Aileen d'Autriche has just remembered that she is the villainess of an otome game — and that her bad ending involves execution. The standard approach would be to lie low, avoid the triggers, and hope for survival.

Aileen is not the lying-low type.

Her analysis of the situation is rapid: the game's true final boss is the demon lord Claude, who eventually destroys the kingdom if the heroine fails to stop him. The heroine, meanwhile, is busy with the game's love interests. If Aileen captures Claude first — makes herself essential to him — she can protect both of them from their respective bad endings.

Her approach to "capturing" the demon lord involves showing up at his castle uninvited, declaring her intentions to him directly, and proceeding to be genuinely useful rather than decorative. Claude, who has spent his life being hated and feared, has no framework for someone who is this aggressively on his side.

## Characters

**Aileen d'Autriche** — The series' defining element — her absolute refusal to be passive, her chaotic energy, and her genuine competence make her the most entertaining villainess protagonist in the genre. She knows the genre, she knows her situation, and she acts rather than reacts.

**Claude** — The demon lord who has never had anyone choose him rather than fear him, whose gradual understanding that Aileen means what she says is the series' emotional core. He is not a brooding obstacle — he is a specific person who has been isolated by what he is.

**The game's heroine** — The girl living out the game's original route, whose relationship with Aileen is more complicated than simple antagonism.

## Art Style

Yuzu's art renders the fantasy setting and court costuming with elegant detail, and Aileen's expressions — her determination, her delight in a well-executed scheme, her genuine moments of vulnerability — are drawn with precise emotional clarity. Claude's visual design is suitably imposing while his expressions when dealing with Aileen provide consistent comedy.

## Cultural Context

The villainess-captures-the-final-boss twist is a specific structural innovation in the genre — it takes the antagonist-and-villain dynamic of most villainess stories and removes the antagonism by having the villainess immediately ally with the most dangerous entity in the game. This eliminates the typical villainess story's tension (avoid the bad ending) and replaces it with a different tension (make Claude believe the alliance is real and worth maintaining).

## What I Love About It

Aileen chose Claude before she knew if her choice would work. She decided he was worth choosing based on her analysis of his situation — not because she fell in love with him first. The series is, at its core, about two people who were both written as obstacles choosing each other instead, and finding that the choice was right.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe this as one of the standout villainess isekai romances — specifically cited for Aileen's specific personality (chaotic, competent, genuinely caring), for Claude's development as more than a romantic object, and for the romance feeling earned through both characters' choices rather than through plot mechanics.

## Memorable Scene ⚠️ Spoiler Warning

The first time Claude does something specifically for Aileen — not because it benefits him, not because she asked, but because he has started to choose her in return — and his complete inability to understand why he made that choice, is the series' most precise moment of the romance becoming mutual.
