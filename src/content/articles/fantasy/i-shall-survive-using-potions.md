## Story Overview

Kaoru Nagase died when a god's transport beam accidentally hit her. As compensation for the error, she received a wish. She asked for a container — a small pouch — that could produce any potion she could imagine, in any quantity, with any property she specified.

She reincarnated in a medieval fantasy world as a young woman. The pouch produces potions. Any potion. She has thought about this carefully: potions that heal, that harm, that alter perception, that create light, that produce effects nobody has classified as a potion before because nobody thought to classify them that way.

She sets up as an apothecary. She builds a network of loyal allies. When problems arrive — and in a medieval fantasy world, problems always arrive — she solves them with the lateral application of potion chemistry and considerable advance planning.

## Characters

**Kaoru Nagase** — Her specific quality is the combination of general competence and specific creativity — she doesn't just have one skill, she has spent time thinking about everything that skill could enable. Her relationships with the people she gathers around her are genuinely affectionate rather than transactional.

**Her Companions** — The young women and various characters who attach themselves to Kaoru form a loyal group that she treats as her household and responsibility. Each has a specific personality and role.

## Art Style

Sukima's art is clean and expressive — Kaoru's expressions convey her internal planning process without extensive internal monologue. The potion effects are depicted with appropriate variety given how many different things they do. The medieval setting is detailed enough to feel grounded.

## Cultural Context

FUNA's isekai works share a specific concern: female protagonists who navigate settings where formal power structures are disadvantageous to them, using indirect means — craft, commerce, relationships — to achieve goals. Kaoru's potion ability fits this pattern: it is not physical power but creative resourcefulness.

## What I Love About It

The sequence where Kaoru faces a political problem that would normally require either military force or aristocratic authority to resolve — and resolves it with a specific potion property combined with advance positioning of allies she's cultivated. The series' most satisfying moments are when her planning reveals how far ahead she was thinking.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers who enjoy FUNA's other works consistently enjoy I Shall Survive Using Potions for its same-author competent-protagonist energy in a more active setting. Kaoru's planning-ahead approach is noted as more engaging than reactive protagonists — readers appreciate being shown that she anticipated the situation three steps ago.

## Memorable Scene ⚠️ Spoiler Warning

The first time Kaoru encounters a problem she had genuinely not anticipated — and her response, rather than panic, is to immediately think about what potions exist or could exist that might address it — demonstrates both her genuine competence and the series' honest treatment of her as someone who can be surprised but not derailed.
