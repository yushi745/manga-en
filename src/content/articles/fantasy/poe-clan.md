## Story Overview

Edgar and his sister Marybell are members of the Poe Clan — vampires who exist outside ordinary time. They can befriend humans, live among them, but cannot stay: Edgar's immortal face will eventually make his lack of aging visible, and they must move on before this happens.

The series follows Edgar across European history — early modern England, Germany, France — in connected episodes that trace his repeated experience of attachment and loss. He befriends humans. They age. He does not. He leaves.

The episodic structure is appropriate: each episode is complete, each loss is complete, and the accumulated weight of these completions is the series' emotional content.

## Characters

**Edgar** — A protagonist whose appearance as a child conceals centuries of experience; the gap between how he looks and what he has lived is the series' visual and emotional center.

**Marybell** — Edgar's sister, whose relationship to their shared immortality is different from his; her trajectory across the series is the most emotionally significant.

## Art Style

Hagio's art is exceptional — the period European settings are rendered with genuine historical attention, and the character designs are distinctive within the aesthetic traditions of 1970s shoujo.

## Cultural Context

Poe Clan ran in Shōjo Comic beginning in 1972. The vampire premise was unusual for the era — Hagio's use of it focused on the emotional and existential dimensions of immortality rather than horror, establishing this register for all subsequent vampire manga.

## What I Love About It

The repetition structure. Edgar befriends someone. They age. He leaves. He befriends someone. They age. He leaves. The series is honest about what immortality actually means: not triumph over death but permanent exposure to it, watching it happen over and over to people you couldn't save.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe Poe Clan as essential to understanding both manga's history and its emotional register — specifically noted for Edgar being one of manga's most affecting immortal characters, for Hagio's art being exceptional despite its age, and for the episodic structure accumulating into something that hits harder than continuous narrative would. Required reading for vampire genre fans and manga historians.
