## Story Overview

In a world where everyone has a skill that defines their social position, Fate Graphite has the Gluttony skill — which activates when he kills something and absorbs its stats. This is considered useless for a gate guard who almost never encounters anything to kill. He is scorned by knights who have combat skills and by nobles who have powerful administrative skills.

After he finally kills an enemy and the Gluttony skill activates, he discovers that absorbing the killed target's statistics makes him immediately stronger than anything he has killed. Each kill makes subsequent kills easier. The power escalates.

The cost: Gluttony's hunger. If Fate does not kill and absorb, the hunger turns inward. Managing the power means managing an escalating need.

## Characters

**Fate Graphite** — His quality is the specific anger of someone who was scorned for something that turned out to be exceptional — and who is aware that the power's usefulness does not retroactively justify how he was treated. His development involves what he does with power that was denied him.

**Roxy Hart** — The noble's daughter who treated him with respect when no one else did, whose history with Fate shapes what his power means to him beyond personal advancement.

## Art Style

FAME's art is the series' most immediately impressive element — the dark fantasy world is rendered with exceptional visual quality, the character designs are distinctive, and the combat sequences combine darkness and dynamism in ways that genre manga typically sacrifices one for the other. The Gluttony ability's visual effects are distinctive.

## Cultural Context

Berserk of Gluttony belongs to the light-novel adaptation wave but approaches the "op protagonist" premise with more darkness than typical — the Gluttony skill's hunger creates an ongoing cost that prevents the power from being simply satisfying.

## What I Love About It

The chapters where the Gluttony skill's hunger becomes threatening — where Fate has to manage how much he kills against how much he needs to kill to keep the hunger at bay — are the series' most interesting content. The power creates a problem that the power is also the only solution to.
