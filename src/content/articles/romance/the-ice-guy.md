## Story Overview

Himuro-kun is a descendant of the Snow Woman. When his emotions intensify — embarrassment, happiness, love — his supernatural heritage manifests: frost on his face, snow falling around him, ice forming on surfaces near him. He is deeply in love with Fuyutsuki-san, his colleague, and she produces more ice than anyone else.

Fuyutsuki-san is not actually cold — she is thoughtful and genuine and cares about him — but she appears composed and reserved in ways that Himuro's ice-producing love misreads as indifference.

The manga follows their slow-burn office romance: Himuro's visible emotional responses, Fuyutsuki's genuine warmth developing into something more explicit, their colleagues' reactions to the literal weather conditions Himuro creates.

## Characters

**Himuro-kun** — A man whose emotions are literally visible through ice; his complete inability to hide how he feels makes him one of romance manga's most transparent and endearing protagonists.

**Fuyutsuki-san** — Appears cold; is genuinely warm; her gradual willingness to express her feelings directly is the romance's development arc.

## Art Style

## What I Love About It

Himuro's honesty. He cannot be dishonest about how he feels — the weather around him is his emotions made visible. Every soft snowfall near Fuyutsuki-san is a declaration; every ice crystal on his face when she smiles at him is a confession.
