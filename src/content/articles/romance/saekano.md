## Story Overview

Tomoya Aki is an otaku who decides to create a dating sim game inspired by a girl he encountered one spring day. He recruits his childhood friend Eriri (visual artist), light novel author Utaha (writer), and the inspiration herself — classmate Megumi Kato, who turns out to be the most ordinary, agreeable, non-reactive person he's ever met. As the creative circle works on the game, the question of who Tomoya actually loves becomes increasingly pointed.

## Characters

The cast of *Saekano: How to Raise a Boring Girlfriend* is built around contrasting personalities that force each other to grow. ## What I Love About It

Megumi Kato's entire arc is the insight that 'ordinary' is not the same as 'empty.' She's ordinary by the standards of rom-com heroines — she doesn't have a dramatic backstory, intense feelings she struggles to express, or a special talent displayed for the protagonist. What she has is genuine presence, genuine care, and the patience to remain herself while everyone around her performs their genre roles. Watching Tomoya realize that this is what he actually needs is the most satisfying romance arc in this genre niche.
