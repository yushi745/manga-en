## Story Overview

Wakana Gojo is learning to make hina dolls in his grandfather's workshop — traditional Japanese craft, painstaking precision, complete social withdrawal after a childhood incident where a friend rejected his world as strange. He does not tell anyone what he is learning.

Marin Kitagawa is popular, openly enthusiastic about the eroge and anime she loves, and completely unashamed of any of it. She discovers Gojo can sew. She asks him to help her make a cosplay costume of her favorite character.

He agrees. The costume is extraordinary. She asks for more.

The series follows Gojo learning, through the experience of making costumes that help Marin become the characters she loves, that his craft has value and that someone finding it wonderful is not strange — it is the beginning of something.

## Characters

**Wakana Gojo** — A protagonist whose withdrawal is specific and psychologically coherent; his growing involvement with Marin's cosplay is tracked as a genuine emotional thaw, not a romantic-comedy convention.

**Marin Kitagawa** — A female lead who is the series' greatest achievement — openly enthusiastic, completely accepting, and specific in her appreciation for exactly what Gojo can do; her warmth is genuine and her directness is refreshing in a genre full of complex romantic games.

## Art Style

Fukuda's art is excellent and improving — the cosplay costumes are drawn with genuine craft knowledge (the effort that goes into each one is visible), and Marin's expressions are among the most expressive in recent romance manga. The ecchi content is handled with the series' characteristic warmth rather than cheap exploitation.

## Cultural Context

My Dress-Up Darling ran in Monthly Young Gangan and was adapted into a popular 2022 anime that significantly expanded the series' readership. Its cultural impact has been significant: Marin became one of the most recognized female characters in recent manga, and the series' model of supportive romance — where the male lead's greatest quality is his genuine investment in the female lead's interests — influenced subsequent romance manga.

## What I Love About It

Gojo does not help Marin to get closer to her. He helps her because she asked and because making her vision real turns out to be the most meaningful work he has done. The romance develops as a consequence of that work rather than as its motivation. That sequencing is exactly right — and it makes the moments when Gojo's feelings become legible to him feel earned rather than convenient.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers consistently describe My Dress-Up Darling as one of the best romance manga of the 2020s — specifically noted for Marin being a rare female lead who is enthusiastic and unashamed rather than restrained and apologetic, for Gojo's support of her interests being the romantic content, and for the cosplay sequences demonstrating genuine craft knowledge. The ecchi content is consistently noted as less intrusive than the T+ rating implies because it is integrated into the craft sequences.

## Memorable Scene ⚠️ Spoiler Warning

The moment Gojo watches Marin wearing the completed costume and understands, specifically, what it means to make something that allows another person to become who they want to be — that is when the series earns everything that follows.
