## Story Overview

At Wataru Fujii's high school, couples wear matching rings. Wataru has a custom ring he loves. His classmate Yuichi Kazuki — school idol, impossibly handsome, cool to everyone — has an identical one. The obvious implication embarrasses both of them. Wataru assumes Kazuki must be insufferably arrogant. Kazuki is actually just deeply reserved and bad at normal social interaction. Their slow, awkward, often painful approach toward understanding each other is the entire story — and it's beautifully told.

## Characters

The cast of *Only the Ring Finger Knows* is built around contrasting personalities that force each other to grow. Supporting characters each serve a distinct emotional function: some mirror the protagonist's flaws, others challenge their assumptions, and a few provide the warmth that makes the harder moments bearable.

## Art Style

Satoru Kannagi's visual style suits the story it tells. Emotional moments land because facial expressions are drawn with real attention to subtlety — you rarely need dialogue to understand what a character is feeling. Background detail varies by scene, pulling back in quiet moments and getting tight and detailed when the stakes rise.
