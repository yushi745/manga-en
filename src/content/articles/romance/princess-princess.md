## Story Overview

Tohru Kouno transfers to Fujimori High School, an elite all-boys school. He's immediately informed of the 'Princess' system: the school's most beautiful students are selected to dress as girls, attend school events in costume, and boost the morale of the student body. Tohru is immediately drafted. He's horrified. His fellow Princess, Yuujirou, has made peace with it. The third, Mikoto, has strong feelings about the whole thing. What follows is a series of chaotic school events and slowly deepening friendships.

## Characters

Mikiyo Tsuda's visual style suits the story it tells. Background detail varies by scene, pulling back in quiet moments and getting tight and detailed when the stakes rise.

## What I Love About It

The bit that makes this manga work is that it takes its ridiculous premise completely seriously. The school system is presented as ordinary administrative procedure. Characters react with genuine exasperation to genuine problems. The comedy comes from the contrast between the absurdity of the situation and everyone's matter-of-fact attitude toward it.
