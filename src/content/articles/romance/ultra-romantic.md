## Story Overview

Tsurezure Ito has extreme shyness that manifests physically — when embarrassed or overwhelmed, his face overheats to dangerous temperatures. Rika Yakumo has no filter — she says what she thinks, directly, without social editing.

They meet. She says things directly that cause his face to overheat. He tries to respond without melting. Their relationship develops through these encounters.

The comedy is in the extremity of their personalities and the physical manifestations of his shyness. The warmth is in their genuine care for each other beneath the comedy.

## Characters

**Tsurezure Ito** — His extreme shyness is a physical condition; the comedy of watching him try to maintain function while his face overheats is the series' most reliable joke.

**Rika Yakumo** — Her directness is complete and non-malicious; she says what she thinks because she genuinely doesn't understand why you wouldn't.

## Art Style

Bolze's art is energetic and expressive — Tsurezure's overheating faces are consistently inventive, and Rika's direct expressions are warm.

## Cultural Context

Ultra Romantic ran in Weekly Shōnen Jump. Jump romantic comedies have a specific tradition of physical comedy with genuine romantic development; this sits comfortably in that tradition.

## What I Love About It

Rika's directness as a superpower. She's not being aggressive or thoughtless — she genuinely says what she thinks because she trusts the person she's talking to enough to be honest. Her directness is a form of respect.
