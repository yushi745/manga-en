## Story Overview

Kazuya Kinoshita uses a rental girlfriend service after being dumped by his first girlfriend. He gets Chizuru Mizuhara — kind, beautiful, professional. When his grandmother is hospitalized and both Kazuya and Chizuru happen to be there, he introduces Chizuru as his real girlfriend. She cannot contradict him in front of his ill grandmother. The lie becomes a commitment.

Chizuru is also his neighbor and a fellow film student pursuing her goal of becoming an actress. Their growing real-life proximity and accumulating emotional history make the pretense increasingly difficult to maintain and increasingly difficult to abandon.

The 25 volumes follow Kazuya's slow growth from cowardly and self-aware about his cowardice to someone capable of genuinely supporting another person, and Chizuru's development from professional to someone with her own feelings she cannot categorize cleanly.

## Characters

**Kazuya Kinoshita** — A protagonist whose internal monologue is the series' distinguishing quality — he narrates his own bad behavior with painful clarity, which makes his eventual growth feel earned rather than sudden.

**Chizuru Mizuhara** — A female lead whose professional composure and personal ambition give her more depth than her initial presentation suggests; her acting career is taken seriously as a goal rather than as character flavoring.

## Art Style

Miyajima's art is clean and character-focused — the character designs are appealing and the romantic scenes are well-staged. The series' comedy relies more on situation and internal monologue than on visual gags.

## Cultural Context

Rent-a-Girlfriend ran in Weekly Shonen Magazine from 2017 to 2023 and was adapted into a popular anime. It arrived during a period of renewed interest in harem comedy manga and distinguished itself through Kazuya's explicit self-awareness — readers who found harem protagonists too oblivious found him alternately refreshing and frustrating.

## What I Love About It

Chizuru wants to be an actress. Her career goal is developed across 25 volumes with genuine attention — auditions, setbacks, small victories — and the series uses it to give the romance a specific stakes. She is not waiting around for Kazuya to figure out his feelings; she has somewhere to be.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers divide sharply on Rent-a-Girlfriend based primarily on Kazuya — readers who find his self-aware cowardice funny and ultimately redeemable rate the series well; readers who find him irredeemably frustrating rate it poorly. Both are responding to the same character. The series is consistent about who Kazuya is and how he grows; whether you want to spend 25 volumes with that growth is the deciding question.
