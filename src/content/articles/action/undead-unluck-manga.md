## Story Overview

Fuuko Izumo has the power of Unluck — her touch causes cascading bad luck for anyone who contacts her. She has been isolated since childhood. After reading a manga with a romantic ending she will never have, she tries to kill herself. An immortal named Andy stops her.

Andy negates death — he cannot die, but he seeks a death grand enough to overcome his negation. He realizes Fuuko's Unluck could theoretically kill him, and proposes partnership.

They are recruited (forcibly) into the Union — an organization of Negators who fight to change the fundamental rules of the world, called the Arbiters, that constrain human existence. The rules include things like death, gravity, and time itself.

The 21 volumes track the Union's escalating war against the Arbiters, Fuuko and Andy's deepening partnership, and Tozuka's increasingly ambitious expansion of the series' conceptual scope.

## Characters

**Fuuko Izumo** — A protagonist whose isolation is genuine and whose partnership with Andy is the first relationship in her life; her development from suicidal to the person willing to fight for the world is tracked with genuine care.

**Andy** — A male lead whose desire for death is the hook and whose actual character — his warmth, his humor, his specific care for Fuuko — is what makes the series work.

## Art Style

Tozuka's art improves substantially across 21 volumes — the early volumes are energetic but rough; the later volumes are confident and inventive. The fight sequences during the final arcs are among the most visually creative in recent Jump.

## Cultural Context

Undead Unluck ran in Weekly Shonen Jump from 2020 to 2024 and was adapted into a 2023 anime. Its conceptual ambition — using the Negator power system to ultimately address the nature of rules themselves — escalated in ways that distinguished it from more formulaic shonen action. The series received the Shogakukan Manga Award in its final year.

## What I Love About It

The series gets bigger and weirder and more ambitious as it goes, and Tozuka earns every escalation. The concepts introduced in volume 1 (Undead, Unluck) are revealed to be the beginning of a conceptual framework that expands to encompass time, reality, and the nature of story itself. That expansion is not arbitrary — it follows from the series' original premise, developed to its logical conclusion.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers consistently describe Undead Unluck as one of the most creative shonen manga of its era — specifically noted for the Negator power system requiring genuine creativity in every fight, for the Fuuko/Andy partnership being one of the warmest central relationships in the genre, and for the series' escalating ambition being consistently earned rather than arbitrary. Frequently cited as the Jump series most likely to surprise readers who assumed they knew what shonen action could do.
