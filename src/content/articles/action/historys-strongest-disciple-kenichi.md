## Story Overview

Kenichi Shirahama is weak. This is established on the first page and maintained rigorously through the first several volumes. He gets beaten up, he runs from fights, and his one genuine skill is an almost inhuman durability — he can take punishment that should break someone his size.

He joins the Ryouzanpaku dojo and meets six masters: Hayato Furinji in Chinese kenpo, Akisame Koetsuji in jujitsu, Kensei Ma in Chinese martial arts, Shio Sakaki in karate, Apachai Hopachai in Muay Thai, and Shigure Kosaka in weapons. Each master is extreme in their discipline and personality. Training under all six simultaneously is physically impossible and psychologically devastating. Kenichi does it anyway.

The series follows his growth from genuinely unable to fight to competent martial artist, while also tracking the masters' own history with a rival organization called YOMI/Yami and the larger conflict those factions represent.

## Characters

The cast of *History's Strongest Disciple Kenichi* is built around contrasting personalities that force each other to grow. Supporting characters each serve a distinct emotional function: some mirror the protagonist's flaws, others challenge their assumptions, and a few provide the warmth that makes the harder moments bearable.

## What I Love About It

Apachai Hopachai teaches children to play. He is the world's most lethal Muay Thai practitioner and he genuinely does not understand that his training is killing his students. Every time he appears in a teaching context, something catastrophic happens. Matsuena makes this funny without making Apachai stupid — he is a genius of violence who has never learned to calibrate for non-lethal contexts.

This is the series in miniature: extreme competence producing catastrophic results for Kenichi, with genuine warmth underneath.
