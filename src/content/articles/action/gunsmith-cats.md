## Story Overview

Rally Vincent runs a gun shop in Chicago with her partner Minnie-May Hopkins. They also do bounty hunting on the side — it pays well and Rally has the skills for it. She knows every firearm by feel, by sound, by mechanism.

The series follows their cases: drug trafficking, arms dealing, the Chicago criminal underground that keeps generating work for licensed bounty hunters with Rally's specific competence.

Minnie-May is younger, cheerful, and handles demolitions. The partnership is the series' consistent pleasure.

## Characters

**Rally Vincent** — A Japanese-American woman who is the most technically knowledgeable gun person in any manga I've read; her competence is demonstrated through action, not stated through exposition.

**Minnie-May Hopkins** — Younger, explosive-oriented, and the series' comedy engine without sacrificing competence; her relationship with Rally is genuine friendship.

## Art Style

Sonoda researched every firearm he drew. The guns in Gunsmith Cats are accurate — the mechanisms, the models, the sounds they make. The action sequences use this accuracy to create choreography that feels real rather than theatrical.

## Cultural Context

Gunsmith Cats required Sonoda to visit Chicago and research both the city and American firearms culture extensively. The result is one of the most specifically American-set Japanese manga — the setting details go beyond surface-level localization. The bounty hunting system, the gun laws, the criminal geography of Chicago are all drawn from research.

## What I Love About It

Rally's competence. She doesn't become skilled through the series — she already is. The pleasure is watching a person who is very good at something operate in situations that require exactly that goodness.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe Gunsmith Cats as the Japanese manga that treats American gun culture with the most accuracy and respect — specifically noted for the firearms research being genuinely impressive, for Rally being one of manga's most competent female protagonists, and for the Chicago setting feeling authentic in ways that surprise readers familiar with the city.

## Memorable Scene ⚠️ Spoiler Warning
