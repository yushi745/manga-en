## Story Overview

Katsu Mukai is an ordinary high schooler who has never particularly applied himself to anything. When he falls for Yuki, whose family runs a boxing gym, he gets pulled into training — first to get close to her, then discovering genuine passion for the sport. The story follows his boxing development alongside his evolving relationship with Yuki, with Adachi's signature blend of sports tension and romantic comedy.

## Characters

Katsu is a classic Adachi protagonist — seemingly ordinary but with hidden qualities that emerge through circumstance. Yuki is warm and capable. The boxing world characters are drawn with affection. The romantic chemistry is understated but real in the Adachi tradition.

## Art Style

Adachi's art is simple, clean, and instantly recognizable — his character designs share DNA across his works in ways that are either charming or distracting depending on the reader. Boxing sequences are clear and action-oriented. The visual storytelling is efficient.

## Cultural Context

Mitsuru Adachi is one of Japan's most beloved sports manga artists, famous for the intertwining of romance and athletic narrative. His work is embedded in Japanese sports manga tradition in ways comparable to how baseball is embedded in Japanese sports culture generally.

## What I Love About It

Adachi's genius is making simple things feel profound. Katsu and Yuki's relationship develops slowly, through small moments, without dramatic declarations. The boxing development proceeds honestly. Nothing is flash. Everything is real. It's easy to underestimate how difficult that is.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Katsu! is well-regarded among Adachi fans as a strong example of his formula. International readers who come to it fresh may find it less emotionally intense than Cross Game or Touch, but its charms are real. Complete at 16 volumes.
