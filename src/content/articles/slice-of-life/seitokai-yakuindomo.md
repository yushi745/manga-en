## Story Overview

Ousai Academy recently became co-ed. Takatoshi Tsuda transfers in and is immediately recruited into the student council because, as a male student, the council needs to show some gender balance. The three female student council members — President Shino Amakusa, Vice President Aria Shichijou, and Secretary Suzu Hagimura — each have distinct comedic personalities.

The series is a joke delivery vehicle. Every interaction produces a punchline. The female characters consistently interpret neutral situations through an adult lens and make explicit commentary. Tsuda responds with the straight-man objection. The joke lands. Repeat.

This is the series across 13 volumes. It does not build toward anything beyond the next joke.

## Characters

**Takatoshi Tsuda** — The straight-man whose reactions define the comedy structure; his reliability as a normal person in an abnormal council is the series' comedic anchor.

**Shino Amakusa** — The student council president whose combination of high academic competence and constant adult humor is the series' central comedic character.

**Aria Shichijou** — The wealthy vice president whose adult humor is delivered with perfect deadpan.

**Suzu Hagimura** — The secretary who is extremely short and extremely intelligent; her sensitivity about her height provides a secondary comedic thread.

## Art Style

Ujiie's art is clean and functional for gag manga — clear character designs, efficient panel layouts that serve joke delivery, and consistent visual timing.

## Cultural Context

Seitokai Yakuindomo ran in Monthly Comic Alive from 2007 to 2022. The series is a gag manga in the tradition of high school slice-of-life comedy, distinguished by the female characters' humor direction rather than female characters' typical shojo-coded responses.

## What I Love About It

The specific inversion. The joke is that the female characters are the ones making the adult jokes and the male character is the scandalized straight man. The format inverts the typical direction of this type of humor, and this inversion generates enough comedic momentum to sustain 13 volumes.

## What English-Speaking Fans Say

Western readers describe Seitokai Yakuindomo as exactly what it is — a reliable gag manga with adult humor that does not pretend to be anything else. Specifically noted for the straight-man structure working consistently, for Suzu being the audience favorite character, and for the series rewarding readers who want volume rather than depth.
