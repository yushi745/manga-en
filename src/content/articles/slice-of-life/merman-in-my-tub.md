## Story Overview

Tatsumi is a practical, serious young man living alone in a small apartment. Wakasa is a vain, cheerful merman who does not want to leave the bathtub Tatsumi put him in.

This is the premise, and the series develops it through domestic accumulation: more sea creatures arrive — an octopus, a jellyfish, a sea slug — each adding to the supernatural household. Wakasa's vanity and his affection for Tatsumi run in parallel, and Tatsumi's long-suffering practicality is consistently tested by requests that no apartment is designed for.

The series does not need more than this to work as a comedic format across 11 volumes.

## Characters

**Tatsumi** — The straight-man whose practical responses to completely impractical situations are the comedy's grounding.

**Wakasa** — The merman whose combination of genuine warmth and total self-absorption makes him a specific kind of comedic character.

**The sea creature visitors** — Each with a different personality appropriate to their species, adding variety to the apartment dynamic.

## Art Style

Itokichi's art captures the specific visual challenge of sea creatures in domestic settings — the bathtub scenes, the various sea creature character designs, and the apartment's gradually overwhelmed state are drawn with warm, clean character work.

## Cultural Context

Merman in My Tub ran in Comic Alive from 2012 to 2019. The series participates in the "supernatural being in domestic space" genre that includes many Japanese slice-of-life manga, distinguishing itself through the sea creature specificity and the boys' love adjacent romantic subtext.

## What I Love About It

The bathtub as ocean. Wakasa treats Tatsumi's bathtub with the same possessive comfort he would treat a proper sea territory. The gap between how Wakasa experiences the bathtub and how Tatsumi experiences it — as his bathroom — is the series' most consistent comedic foundation.
